**In the first half of 2026, 1,220 products launched on aat.ee, across a catalog of 1,445 live products.** Here's what that data says about how much founders are shipping, when, and in which categories — and where AI actually sits in the mix.

> Figures cover launches dated January 1 – June 2026 on aat.ee, as of publication. June is partial. Methodology is at the end.

## Launch cadence

Launch volume stayed remarkably steady through the half — roughly 200 launches a month, with a dip in April and a strong late-spring rebound:

| Month          | Launches |
| -------------- | -------- |
| January        | 200      |
| February       | 232      |
| March          | 193      |
| April          | 116      |
| May            | 240      |
| June (partial) | 239      |

The April dip and the May–June surge are the standout movements; otherwise founders shipped at a consistent ~200/month clip.

## What founders are building

Launches by category (a product can span more than one category, so these overlap):

| Category                | Launches (H1 2026) |
| ----------------------- | ------------------ |
| Artificial Intelligence | 543                |
| Productivity            | 327                |
| Developer Tools         | 226                |
| SaaS                    | 217                |
| Marketing Tools         | 159                |
| Design Tools            | 91                 |
| Open Source             | 63                 |
| APIs & Integrations     | 51                 |

## The takeaways

- **AI is nearly half of everything.** At 543 launches, Artificial Intelligence touches about 45% of H1 launches — comfortably the largest category and the throughline across most others.
- **Builders are building for builders.** Developer Tools (226), SaaS (217), and APIs & Integrations (51) show how much of the activity is infrastructure and tooling, not just end-user apps.
- **Productivity stays evergreen.** At 327, productivity remains the second-largest category — a reminder that "help people get more done" never goes out of style.
- **Steady beats spiky.** A consistent ~200 launches/month suggests a healthy, continuous pipeline rather than a few viral moments.

For founders, the signal is twofold: the categories above are crowded, so **distribution and discoverability matter as much as the product** — and with AI saturating the field, being the tool an assistant _names_ is a real edge (see our [GEO/AIEO guide](/blog/geo-aieo-get-recommended-by-ai)).

## Methodology

Counts are drawn from aat.ee's own launch records: products with a launched status and a scheduled launch date between 2026-01-01 and the end of June 2026. June is partial (through publication). Category totals count a product once per category it belongs to, so they sum to more than the total launch count. "Live products" counts everything currently launched or ongoing.
