**A dofollow directory backlink is a link from a directory that passes ranking authority (it isn't marked `nofollow`) — and in 2026 these are still one of the cleanest, most repeatable ways for a SaaS to build authority, get indexed faster, and show up when AI assistants recommend tools.** The catch: not all directories are worth it. This guide covers what "dofollow" really means, why directory links still work, and how to tell a useful directory from a waste of time.

## What "dofollow" actually means

When a site links to you, that link either passes authority or it doesn't:

- **Dofollow** — the default. Search engines follow it and pass "link equity" (ranking signal) to your site.
- **Nofollow / ugc / sponsored** — a `rel` attribute telling search engines _not_ to pass authority. The link can still send traffic, but it doesn't help your rankings.

Many big directories make their outbound links `nofollow`, which is why a link being **dofollow** matters so much: it's the difference between a link that builds your domain's authority and one that's cosmetic.

## Why directory backlinks still work in 2026

Three reasons, and they compound:

1. **Authority.** A dofollow link from a relevant, reputable directory passes real ranking signal — especially valuable for a new SaaS with few backlinks.
2. **Indexing speed.** Search engines (and AI crawlers) discover new pages by following links from pages they already crawl often. A listing on an already-indexed directory gets your product found in days, not weeks.
3. **AI visibility (GEO).** Directories are exactly the kind of source LLMs lean on to learn "what are the leading tools in category X." A dofollow listing feeds both Google and the models — see our [GEO/AIEO guide](/blog/geo-aieo-get-recommended-by-ai).

## What makes a directory link worth getting

Not all directories are equal. Look for:

| Signal                 | Why it matters                                                                              |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dofollow**           | Passes authority instead of being cosmetic                                                  |
| **Domain Rating (DR)** | Higher-DR domains pass more weight — see [what DR is](/blog/domain-rating-dr-ai-visibility) |
| **Relevance**          | A tech/product directory beats a generic link farm                                          |
| **Already indexed**    | If Google has crawled it, your listing gets found fast                                      |
| **Permanent**          | A link that disappears next month does nothing long-term                                    |

A handful of links that clear this bar beat hundreds of thin, nofollow, never-indexed ones.

## Common mistakes

- **Chasing nofollow giants only.** Traffic is nice, but if nothing is dofollow you're building no authority.
- **Ignoring relevance.** An off-topic link is worth far less than an on-topic one.
- **Inconsistent details.** Different product name/URL/description across listings weakens the signal — keep them identical.
- **One-and-done, then it vanishes.** Prefer directories that keep your listing permanently live.

## FAQ

**Are directory backlinks still safe in 2026?**
Yes — _relevant, quality_ ones are. What's risky is mass-submitting to spammy, irrelevant link farms. Stick to reputable, on-topic directories.

**Dofollow vs nofollow — does nofollow ever help?**
Nofollow links can drive referral traffic and brand exposure, but they don't pass ranking authority. For SEO impact you want dofollow.

**How do I know a directory's link is dofollow?**
Check the link's `rel` attribute (inspect the listing), or use a backlink tool. aat.ee's network listings are dofollow by design.

**How fast will I see results?**
Indexing/discovery can happen within days; ranking authority builds over weeks as the link is crawled and trusted.
