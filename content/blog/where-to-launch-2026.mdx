**Product Hunt is still a great launch venue, but in 2026 it's one option among many — and the founders who get the most from a launch don't bet on a single platform.** The right move is to pick venues by what they actually give you: the right audience, a durable dofollow backlink, authority (DR), AI visibility, and a slot that fits your timeline. Here's how to choose.

## Product Hunt: what it's good (and not) for

Product Hunt is strong for a concentrated day-one spike of early-adopter attention and feedback. What it's less reliable for: lasting SEO value (many links are nofollow), evergreen discovery after launch day, and guaranteed reach if the day is crowded. Treat it as one high-energy moment — not your whole distribution.

## Why launch on more than one platform

A single launch day is a spike; discovery should compound. Listing across multiple relevant venues:

- multiplies the audiences you reach,
- earns [dofollow backlinks](/blog/dofollow-directory-backlinks-saas) that keep building authority,
- and seeds the sources AI assistants pull from ([GEO/AIEO](/blog/geo-aieo-get-recommended-by-ai)).

## How to evaluate a launch platform

| Criterion                  | Why it matters                                                                         |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Audience fit**           | The right users beat a bigger but irrelevant crowd                                     |
| **Dofollow backlink**      | Builds lasting authority, not just a one-day visit                                     |
| **Domain Rating (DR)**     | Higher-DR venues pass more weight — [what DR is](/blog/domain-rating-dr-ai-visibility) |
| **AI / search visibility** | Does a listing help you get found (and named) after launch day?                        |
| **Queue / scheduling**     | Can you launch when you're ready, or wait in line?                                     |
| **Cost**                   | Free vs paid — and what the paid tier actually adds                                    |

Score your candidates on these rather than chasing the biggest name.
